If you live in Cranberry Township, Zelienople, Wexford, or anywhere in Butler County, one of the most common questions homeowners ask is: How much does it cost to hire a painter near me?

Painting is one of the fastest ways to refresh a home — but pricing varies depending on the size of the job, prep work, and the condition of your walls. Here's what homeowners in Western PA typically pay in 2026.

Average Interior Painting Costs in Western PA

Most homeowners in Cranberry pay:

Job TypeTypical Cost
Per square foot$2.00–$4.50
Single room$300–$700
Full interior repaint$1,800–$4,500

Factors That Raise the Price

  • Old walls needing patching before painting
  • Water damage or stains requiring primer coats
  • Dark colors requiring multiple coats
  • High ceilings or stairwells requiring ladders
Local note: Homes in neighborhoods like Glen Eden, Pinehurst, and Seven Fields often have two-story foyers that require ladders and extra labor — factor this in when getting estimates.

Average Exterior Painting Costs in Western PA

Home SizeTypical Cost
Most homes$2,500–$7,500
Larger homes / older siding$10,000+
Local note: Western PA's freeze-thaw cycles and humidity mean siding needs more prep work than in warmer states. Expect thorough scraping, caulking, and priming to be part of any quality exterior job.

What Affects Painting Costs?

  • Amount of prep work — patching, sanding, caulking
  • Paint quality — Sherwin-Williams and Benjamin Moore cost more but last longer
  • Number of coats required
  • Trim, doors, and baseboards — usually priced separately
  • Furniture moving — some painters include it, some don't

When Should You Hire a Painter?

Call a painter if you notice:

  • Peeling or bubbling paint — interior or exterior
  • Water stains on walls or ceilings
  • Cracked drywall or damaged surfaces
  • Faded exterior siding or trim
  • Scuff marks and wear that won't clean off
