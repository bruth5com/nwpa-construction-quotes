Renovating Franklin & Oil City's Older Homes

Franklin and Oil City are full of character homes from another era, and renovating them takes a different approach than a standard remodel. We connect you with contractors who know how to update wiring, plumbing and layouts in an older home without losing the details that make it worth keeping.

Whether you're modernizing systems, opening up a floor plan, or restoring original trim and woodwork, your contractor will assess what's original, what needs updating, and what can be preserved before giving you an estimate.

What's Included

  • Electrical and plumbing modernization for older homes
  • Structural repairs — foundations, floor joists, load-bearing changes
  • Trim, woodwork and plaster restoration
  • Layout updates that respect the original architecture
  • Whole-house renovations for historic properties

Local note: Homes built before the 1950s in this area often have knob-and-tube wiring or galvanized plumbing still in place — a contractor experienced with older homes will flag this during the walkthrough, not mid-renovation.

Common Questions

Will renovating my older home uncover surprises?

Sometimes — older homes can reveal outdated wiring, plumbing or structural issues once walls are opened. A good contractor will build a contingency into your estimate and communicate clearly if something unexpected comes up.

Can I keep original trim and woodwork while updating the rest?

Yes — many contractors in our network specialize in preserving original details like trim, doors and flooring while modernizing what's behind the walls.
